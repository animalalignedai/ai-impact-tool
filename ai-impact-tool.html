Important context: At an individual level, AI water use is a small fraction of daily life — the average person uses ~200L/day. The concern is the aggregate: 700M+ queries per day globally means freshwater evaporation matching the annual drinking needs of 1.2 million people.

Scope 1 = direct cooling water at the data center. Scope 1+2 = includes water used by the power grid to generate electricity. OpenAI reported 0.32 mL per query (scope 1 only); when UC Riverside researchers included scope 2, figures rose to 10–50 mL per medium response. Figures here use a mid-range scope 1+2 estimate of ~15 mL per query. Sources: Li et al. / "Making AI Less Thirsty" (2023, updated 2025); WaterFreeChat (2026); arXiv 2505.09598.

About this tool

Created by Centre for Animal-Aligned AI with assistance from Claude (Anthropic).  ·  Built for nonprofit advocacy teams.  ·  Data sources: UNESCO, UC Riverside, EpochAI, OpenAI, Google, arXiv 2505.09598 & 2509.20241.  ·  Figures are estimates — exact consumption varies by model, hardware, query length, and data center location.

⚡ Transparency: energy used to build this tool

This tool was developed through an extended conversation with Claude Sonnet (a large language model), involving approximately 15–20 back-and-forth exchanges covering research, visual design, and iteration. Based on published estimates of ~0.34–1 Wh per query for frontier-scale models, the total energy used to build this tool is estimated at roughly 5–20 Wh — equivalent to leaving an LED bulb on for 30 minutes to 2 hours, or driving an electric car approximately 65–265 feet.

We share this in the spirit of the transparency we advocate for. AI tools have a real, if modest, per-session footprint — and the goal of this resource is to help organizations make more intentional choices, not to suggest AI use should be avoided entirely. The research and educational value of tools like this can justify their cost when used thoughtfully.
